Client

When deciding on a webpage for myself, I wanting something practical. Something I can actually use in real life to demstrate my skills and accomplishments. I debated exactly what I wanted the site to be, but eventually decided on a page to show off my theatre accomplishments. I used AI to help with some ideas for the page, and landed on a home page where I could introduce myself and my acting philosophy, a gallery page to show photos of productions I've been in, and a script archive where I can include my favorite scripts and monolougues. This gives me one place to turn for audition material, as well as a sort of resume to show potential directors. The lead and junior developers did a really good job putting the site together. There were no issues with communication. I could nitpick it (images load slowly, and a couple images are off center), but overall they exceeded my expectations. If I were to actually hire a team for a site I would try to be more involved. Checking up on progress and nitpicking the final product.

In Character

Lead Developer

I was tasked with designing a ticket sales page for Justin Rodriguez called BandLive. I used teams to communicate with both my juniors and the client and that is where we shared progress and the link to the page. Things went fairly smoothly. His wireframe took a second to figure out, but I was able to design the page without any issues after that. There was a little bit of a miscommunication with the Client about getting access to the images he wanted on the page, but it was quickly figured out. The CSS was definately harder than the HTML, but it did come together eventually. My junior developers were pretty on top of their work as well. There was some miscommunication about how I was getting their code and about designing the CSS for the sub pages. The developer for the Pop page designed his own CSS from the ground up rather than using mine as a base, and the designer for the Rock page just used my CSS without making any adjustments. I ended up having to edit the CSS for the Pop page, putting it into my CSS and removing anything that didn't work with mine to make it uniform. The Rock page ended up looking OK so I just left it. Using Github was confusing for me at first, but once I got help in class things went smoothly.

General Reflections

Overall, I enjoyed this assignment. It was kind of fun figuring out how to build a real website on my own. The most difficult part was largly communication. Understanding what the client wanted and how to impliment it could be tricky and sometime I had to wait for responses from my lead developers before I could continue my work. If I were to do such a project again, I would love to sit down with the whole team in person at the beginning of the project and go over it together so expectations were set. I definately feel like I have learned in this class. Taking on such a project again without in-class help feel rather intimidating. I'm not 100% confident I could do it on my own, but I would be willing to try.
